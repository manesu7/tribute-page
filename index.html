Aznavour's Honors

    Aznavour was born on 1924, May 22 in Paris in an armenian family of artists. He was named Shahnour (or Chahnour) Vaghinag Aznavourian (Armenian: Շահնուր Վաղինակ Ազնավուրեան), by his parents, Armenian immigrants Michael Aznavourian and Knar Baghdasarian. His father sang in restaurants in France before establishing a Caucasian restaurant called Le Caucase. Charles's parents introduced him to performing at an early age, and he dropped out of school at age nine, and took the stage name "Aznavour."

  • 1995 – Grand Medal of the French Academy.
  • 1997 – Officier (Officer) of the Legion of Honour.
  • 2004 – Commandeur (Commander) of the Legion of Honour.
  • 2004 – National Hero of Armenia.
  • 2004 – Officer of the Order of Leopold.
  • 2008 – Honorary Officer of the Order of Canada.
  • 2008 – Citizenship of Armenia.
  • 2009 – Officer of the National Order of Quebec.
  • 2015 – Commander in the Belgian Order of the Crown.
  • 2017 – Raoul Wallenberg Medal.
